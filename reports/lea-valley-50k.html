Spring 2025  ·  Camino Ultra  ·  Welwyn Garden City to Hackney Wick

Lea Valley 50k

4:31 Finish time
3rd Female
First Ultra ever

I was supposed to run this race in 2024. I'd been building well — hitting 100km weeks, 5k times coming down — and then a dull pain appeared on top of my tibia that wouldn't go away with rest. I could feel it at night. Pressing on that specific spot was unbearable. I kept running through it for about two weeks, convinced it was soft tissue, because stress fractures can warm up slightly and you can push through them — right up until you can't.

A word to anyone in that position: a pain that won't warm off, hurts at night, and has one exact pressure point is not a soft tissue injury. Stop running. I didn't, and I paid for it with most of 2024.

So 2025 was finally the year. Three or four weeks before the London Marathon — not the most sensible timing, but I wasn't missing it again.

Getting there

Public transport to the start, which went fine except for the part where I got lost and had to follow another runner to find the venue. The start is at Gosling Sports Centre in Welwyn Garden City — a genuinely lovely setting. First race in my life where I didn't have to queue for the toilets. The men's queue, on the other hand, was a different story.

I took a nervous photo at the start. I was definitely nervous. I know some people say a 50k is just a long marathon, but it wasn't just a long marathon to me — it was something I'd been trying to get to for over a year.

The race

My plan was negative splits: start stupidly easy, pick up the pace at the end. No time goal, just finish and finish well. I stayed with a group through the town section where navigation needed a bit of attention, then hit the canal and went at my own thing. The canal is straightforward — I have a complete lack of directional sense and even I managed it.

Running with people, even briefly, reminded me of when I first started. I do all my training alone, and there's something about company on a long run that changes the whole feeling of it.

I didn't stop at the first checkpoint — didn't need to. Took water at the second and third. By then it was getting hot — first proper warm day of the year — and I was starting to cook. And then I broke my water bottle. I still don't know how. The last 5k: hungry, thirsty, surrounded by people on a sunny London day, everyone shirtless, the smell from the boats...'I really could do with a sandwich' I thought.

The finish

You can't see the finish line from the canal, which means you're running on faith until a marshal appears holding a sign. When I crossed the line someone told me I was third female. I spent a very long time next to the person with the water jug, drinking until I physically couldn't drink any more.

What worked

The negative splits plan. Starting easy and building actually worked — the legs held up.

The route. Welwyn to Hackney Wick via the Lea Valley — I don't find canal running boring. The change of scenery, the water, the gradual shift from rural Hertfordshire to East London. Lovely.

Point to point. Love a point-to-point. You are always moving towards something.

What didn't

The timing. Three weeks before London Marathon is too close. I knew it going in and did it anyway.

The bottle. Don't break your water bottle with 5k to go on a hot day. Operational error.

The lime bikes. No further comment needed.

My first ultra. Third female. I spent 2024 injured and watching this race happen without me. Worth the wait.
