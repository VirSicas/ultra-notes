This one had been on my radar for a while, mostly for one reason: it starts close to home and ends in Richmond. There's something appealing about a race that begins as a normal Saturday and ends 55k later in a different part of your own city.

After London marathon, I hadn't built a block for this. It wasn't a target race, it was an adventure — something to do because it sounded fun, not because I had a time in mind.

The start

Same nerves as always at the start line. Everyone looks like they were born to do this, and you stand there wondering what you're doing among them. Runners were split into batches based on predicted finish time, with one eye on the forecast. I put myself down for around 5:30, which in hindsight says more about my optimism than my preparation. I'd more or less ignored the elevation profile, and I definitely hadn't thought through what running with no shade would feel like once the heat picked up.

Going well, until it wasn't

I took it easy and felt genuinely good for most of the race. Then, at 40k, my vision blurred. I stopped, heart racing, properly dizzy. I knew straight away what it was — a sugar crash — which was almost funny given I'd just left a checkpoint where I'd had coke, food, sweets, the works. None of it had landed in time.

My brain wasn't working well enough to problem-solve, so I called my partner and asked him what I should do. I'm aware of how that sounds. Ten miles to go, mid-crash, on the phone asking for instructions like a child who's lost their parent in a supermarket.

The long walk back

Point-to-point races don't really give you an easy way out, and logistically, finishing in Richmond was a far better route home than trying to abandon somewhere in the middle of nowhere. So the plan became: walk until I feel human again, try to eat, and hope the crash passes.

It didn't. I got increasingly disoriented. Eventually I found a shop and bought a coke — a drink I genuinely don't like — and it saved me. I got another 5k out of that. Then another coke. Then one more, until the finish line finally showed up.

The finish

I crossed the line in 6:42, a long way from my naive 5:30 guess, but upright and, eventually, lucid again.

Takeaways

The route itself is lovely and I'd recommend the race without hesitation. What I need to take from it: I don't understand my own fuelling well enough yet, and I clearly do not run well in the heat. Give me a cold, miserable, grey day over this any time.

Wins

Thing that worked. The route is very well marked.

Damage report

What hurt. Nothing really, body felt fine.

What didn't work. Nutrition. Not sure what I did wrong, but need to work on that.

This race is a no for me as I don't do well in the heat.
