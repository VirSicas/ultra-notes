Ultra London 50k

5:05:48 Finish time
1st W40 category
3rd Female
5th Overall

I signed up for this one as soon as it appeared — running 50k through London sounded like a brilliant way to actually see the city. What I failed to notice was how soon after the London Marathon it was. Tired legs, carbon plates I'm not used to, and a watch I'd never tested the route-following feature on. Ideal preparation.

Health before times is the new motto. I was nervous at the start.

The atmosphere

Once I arrived, things felt immediately better. The same welcoming energy I'd felt at Lea Valley — no clubs, no times, just people who want to run far and not be horrible about it. There's something about these smaller ultras that big road races simply can't replicate.

I found Simon — we'd met at the one recce I actually managed to do. He was nervous too. We bonded over nerves, I lent him a pen, borrowed his suncream. The whole place smelled like Spanish holidays.

Getting lost (immediately)

We set off in waves to avoid congestion. Somehow I ended up in the last one — still not entirely sure how. I started following the stickers. Until I wasn't. There were some random arrows on the floor at some point, and that was enough to get me thoroughly confused.

By 7k my watch was lost, I was off course, and I was texting my partner considering whether this was going to be a DNF. I made it to Crystal Palace and then Kelsey- a Camino Angel. A familiar face appeared and helped me find my way back. New strategy from that point: stick with anyone who looks like they know where they're going.

Mark, and the first half

I found Mark — a local, no vest, just carrying a bottle of water, barely glancing at a map. An absolute legend. We chatted the whole way and I didn't have to think about navigation once. Running with someone who knows a route is a completely different experience; the anxiety just drops away and you can actually run.

The three amigos

Second half: Leigh and Simon. What an adventure. We took turns pacing, made tactical Tesco stops when the heat got serious, and got chased by a dog. I'm counting all of that as race strategy.

It was very hot, and I do very badly in the heat. They genuinely saved me in that second half — not just pace-wise but the company of it. Running through London in the sun with people who are equally committed to finishing and equally unserious about how you get there.

The final stretch through Parliament was too much. My brain had genuinely stopped working by then and I was very happy when it was over. But we finished together.

What worked

Finding people. Mark for the first half, Leigh and Simon for the second. The whole thing only worked because of them.

The route. Crystal Palace, Parliament, parts of London I'd never have seen otherwise. That was the whole point of signing up and it delivered.

Tactical Tesco stops. Underrated race strategy.

Finishing 1st W40 while half-delirious in the heat. Still counts.

What didn't

Timing. Too soon after London Marathon. Know your calendar before you sign up.

The watch. Test your kit before race day. Getting lost at 7k on an unfamiliar route feature is entirely avoidable.

The heat. I do not run well in it. This is a pattern. I keep ignoring it.

Chaotic, hot, and one of my favourite days of running. Would do it again — with a working watch and fewer marathons in my legs.
